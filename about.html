Our Background

The corporation's name was inspired by the word philanthropist that derived from the greek word philos meaning “love” and brew meaning “to pour hot water over fresh coffee grounds.” As the corporation's mission implies, we diligently strive to provide our customers with the highest-quality cup of coffee while supporting the nation's local farmers for the betterment of the economy.

Discover

Our Vision and Mission

VISION
Philos Brew Inc. seeks to be the greatest coffee shop in the nation while maintaining its uncompromising principle of helping local coffee farmers by giving each customer a wonderful and delicious coffee everyday.

MISSION
Philos Brew Inc. diligently strive to provide its customers with the highest-quality cup of coffee while supporting the nation's local farmers for the betterment of the economy. This company draws inspiration from the tenacity and indigeneity of our local coffee farmers and aim to achieve this mission by acquiring farmer-direct coffee beans to assist the livelihood of Filipino farmers in the Philippines.

Our Logo

STEAM FORMING A HAND - Illustrates that the Philos Brew's dedication in helping the country's local farmers in order to boost the economy is carried out with every cup of premium coffee it makes.
COFFEE CUP - Simply shows coffee, the primary product that Philos Brew offers to the general public.
FIVE STARS - This is referring to the quality of drinks that Philos Brew will provide to potential customers.
GREEK LETTERS - The Greek letters form the word "philos."
CRESCENT SHAPE - The crescent moon symbolizes fertility, in which agriculture and fertility may be associated in some way. The capability to sustain agricultural plant growth is referred to as fertility. Therefore, it aims to symbolize the consistent, high-quality, and sustained harvests from local farmers.

Our Production

Philos Brew promotes products grown by our local farmers.
Locally grown coffee beans from different areas in the Philippines such as in Batangas, Cavite, and in different parts of Luzon and Mindanao are what we mostly use for the products that we offer.

Our Economic Goals

Poverty is defined as a situation in which a family's fundamental necessities, such as food, housing, clothes, and education, are still not met. It can result in additional issues such as low proficiency, poverty, hunger, and so on. Thus, We all have a responsibility to work toward upcoming years where there are fewer starving kids and fewer impoverished individuals. We must build a nation with powerful remote communities, an educated society, and a bright future.
As a result, Philos Brew will examine potential charity causes and choose one or two actively engaged on a regular basis by donating a certain proportion of our annual revenue.
